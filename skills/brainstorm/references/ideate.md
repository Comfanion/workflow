# Ideate Methods

Methods for generating creative solutions to well-defined problems.

## Brainstorming

**Purpose:** Generate a large quantity of diverse ideas

**When to use:**
- After defining the problem (HMW questions ready)
- When you need many options
- When team is stuck on one approach
- Early in solution exploration

**Rules:**
1. **Defer judgment** - No criticism during ideation
2. **Quantity over quality** - Aim for volume
3. **Wild ideas welcome** - Encourage crazy thinking
4. **Build on others' ideas** - "Yes, and..."
5. **Stay focused** - One conversation at a time
6. **Be visual** - Sketch, don't just talk

**How to run:**

1. **Setup (5 min):**
   - State the HMW question
   - Review the rules
   - Set timer (15-20 min)

2. **Generate (15-20 min):**
   - Each person writes ideas (one per sticky note)
   - Share and build on each other's ideas
   - Facilitator keeps energy high
   - Aim for 30-50 ideas

3. **Cluster (10 min):**
   - Group similar ideas
   - Name each cluster
   - Identify themes

4. **Vote (5 min):**
   - Each person gets 3 votes
   - Vote on most promising ideas
   - Top ideas move to prototyping

**Example Session:**

```markdown
HMW: "How might we reduce merchant anxiety during bank detail entry?"

Ideas Generated:
1. Show security badges and certifications
2. Explain why each field is needed (inline)
3. Let merchants skip and come back later
4. Show how many merchants have completed this step
5. Use progressive disclosure (ask less upfront)
6. Offer phone verification as alternative
7. Show a video of someone completing the step
8. Add a live chat widget for this specific step
9. Let merchants enter a test transaction first
10. Show what happens after they complete this step
11. Use familiar payment provider branding (Stripe, PayPal)
12. Offer to pre-fill from existing business accounts
13. Add a "why we need this" expandable section
14. Show a trust seal from a known authority
15. Let merchants call and dictate info over phone

Top Voted: #3 (skip), #5 (progressive disclosure), #13 (why we need this)
```

**Output:** 30-50 ideas, top 3-5 selected for prototyping

## SCAMPER

**Purpose:** Systematic idea generation by applying 7 thinking lenses

**When to use:**
- When brainstorming stalls
- To improve existing solutions
- When you need structured creativity
- To explore all angles

**The 7 Lenses:**

### 1. Substitute
What can you replace?
- Different input method?
- Different data source?
- Different technology?
- **Example:** Replace typed bank details with photo of check

### 2. Combine
What can you merge?
- Combine steps?
- Combine with another product?
- Combine features?
- **Example:** Combine identity verification with bank verification

### 3. Adapt
What can you borrow from elsewhere?
- From other industries?
- From competitors?
- From nature?
- **Example:** Adapt the "instant approval" model from credit cards

### 4. Modify
What can you change in size, shape, or form?
- Make it bigger/smaller?
- Make it faster/slower?
- Change the order?
- **Example:** Modify the form to show only 1 field at a time

### 5. Put to Other Uses
Can you use it differently?
- Different context?
- Different user?
- Different purpose?
- **Example:** Use the onboarding flow as a store setup wizard

### 6. Eliminate
What can you remove?
- Remove steps?
- Remove fields?
- Remove decisions?
- **Example:** Eliminate bank details until first sale

### 7. Reverse
What if you did the opposite?
- Reverse the order?
- Reverse the roles?
- Reverse assumptions?
- **Example:** Reverse: let the platform pay the merchant first (trust gesture)

**Example SCAMPER Session:**

```markdown
Problem: "Merchants abandon onboarding at bank detail entry"

| Lens | Idea |
|------|------|
| **Substitute** | Replace manual entry with Plaid bank connection |
| **Combine** | Combine bank setup with first product listing |
| **Adapt** | Adapt Shopify's "start selling, add bank later" model |
| **Modify** | Show only 2 fields at a time instead of all 8 |
| **Put to Other Uses** | Use bank verification as a trust signal for buyers |
| **Eliminate** | Remove bank details until merchant makes first sale |
| **Reverse** | Platform sends $0.01 to verify bank (they verify us) |
```

**Output:** 7+ ideas, one per lens

## Crazy 8s

**Purpose:** Rapidly sketch 8 different solutions in 8 minutes

**When to use:**
- When you need visual solutions
- To push past obvious ideas
- When team has analysis paralysis
- After initial brainstorming

**How to do it:**

1. **Fold paper into 8 sections**
2. **Set timer for 8 minutes** (1 minute per section)
3. **Sketch one idea per section**
4. **Rules:**
   - No erasing, no perfection
   - Each sketch must be different
   - Words are OK (labels, annotations)
   - Stick figures are fine

5. **Share and discuss:**
   - Each person presents their 8 ideas (1 min each)
   - Team votes on favorites
   - Combine best elements

**Tips:**
- Ideas 1-3 are usually obvious
- Ideas 4-8 are where creativity happens
- The time pressure forces fast thinking
- Don't worry about drawing skill

**Example:**

```markdown
Crazy 8s: "Bank Detail Entry Screen"

1. Traditional form (all fields visible)
2. Wizard-style (one field at a time)
3. Chat-bot guided entry
4. Photo capture of bank document
5. Connect via Plaid/bank API
6. Voice-guided entry
7. Video call with support agent who helps
8. Skip entirely - earn store credit instead
```

**Output:** 8 rough sketches per person, top ideas selected

## Provotype Sketching

**Purpose:** Explore extreme versions of solutions to find the right balance

**When to use:**
- When you're not sure how far to push an idea
- To challenge assumptions
- To find the right scope
- When team disagrees on approach

**How to do it:**

1. **Pick a concept** (e.g., "simplified onboarding form")
2. **Create extreme versions:**

```markdown
## Provotype: Onboarding Form

### Extreme 1: Zero-Field Form
- No form at all
- Just "Start Selling" button
- Collect info later, as needed
- **Provokes:** What info do we really need upfront?

### Extreme 2: 100-Field Form
- Ask for everything possible
- Full business plan, tax details, references
- Background check, credit score
- **Provokes:** What's the cost of asking too much?

### Extreme 3: Voice-Only Form
- No typing at all
- Speak your answers
- AI fills in the form
- **Provokes:** Is the keyboard the problem?

### Extreme 4: Collaborative Form
- Merchant fills it out with a support agent
- Screen share, co-browsing
- Real-time help
- **Provokes:** Is the problem loneliness/confusion?

### Extreme 5: Gamified Form
- Each field completed = points
- Progress bar, achievements
- Unlock features as you go
- **Provokes:** Is the problem motivation?
```

3. **Discuss each extreme:**
   - What's good about this extreme?
   - What's terrible about it?
   - What element could we keep?

4. **Find the sweet spot:**
   - Combine best elements from extremes
   - Define the "just right" solution

**Output:** Extreme concepts, insights about boundaries, refined solution direction

## Analogous Inspiration

**Purpose:** Borrow solutions from unrelated domains

**When to use:**
- When industry solutions feel stale
- To find truly novel approaches
- When the problem exists in other contexts
- To break out of domain thinking

**How to do it:**

1. **Abstract the problem:**
   - Remove domain-specific language
   - Find the universal challenge

2. **Find analogies:**
   - What other domains solve this?
   - How does nature solve it?
   - How did historical solutions work?

3. **Extract principles:**
   - What makes the analogy work?
   - What principle can we borrow?

4. **Apply to your domain:**
   - How would this principle work here?

**Examples:**

```markdown
Problem: "How to build trust with new merchants"

### Analogy 1: Nature - Symbiosis
How do organisms build trust?
- Start with small exchanges
- Gradually increase investment
- Both parties benefit
**Applied:** Start with small features, unlock more as trust builds

### Analogy 2: Games - Tutorial Levels
How do games teach new players?
- Start with simple actions
- Gradually increase complexity
- Celebrate achievements
- Can't fail (safety net)
**Applied:** Onboarding as tutorial with achievements and safety nets

### Analogy 3: Restaurants - Tasting Menu
How do restaurants build trust with new diners?
- Offer samples before commitment
- Start with small portions
- Build to main course
**Applied:** Let merchants try features before full setup

### Analogy 4: GPS Navigation
How does GPS handle uncertainty?
- Shows the full route upfront
- Gives step-by-step guidance
- Recalculates when you go off-path
- Shows estimated time remaining
**Applied:** Show full onboarding path, step-by-step with ETA
```

**Output:** 3-5 analogies with applied principles

## When to Use Each Method

| Method | Best For | Time | Output |
|--------|----------|------|--------|
| **Brainstorming** | Volume of ideas | 30-45 min | 30-50 ideas |
| **SCAMPER** | Structured exploration | 30-45 min | 7+ ideas |
| **Crazy 8s** | Visual solutions, speed | 15-20 min | 8 sketches/person |
| **Provotypes** | Finding boundaries | 30-60 min | Extreme concepts |
| **Analogous Inspiration** | Novel approaches | 30-45 min | 3-5 analogies |

**Typical Workflow:**
```
Brainstorming (diverge) -> SCAMPER (explore) -> Crazy 8s (visualize) -> Vote -> Prototype
```

## Tips

**Brainstorming:**
- Warm up with an unrelated creative exercise
- Use "Yes, and..." to build on ideas
- Capture EVERYTHING (even bad ideas spark good ones)
- Separate generation from evaluation

**SCAMPER:**
- Go through all 7 lenses (don't skip any)
- Spend 3-5 minutes per lens
- The best ideas often come from Eliminate and Reverse

**Crazy 8s:**
- Don't draw on the first section until timer starts
- If stuck, combine two previous ideas
- Ideas 4-8 are usually the most interesting
- Share all 8, not just your favorite

**Provotypes:**
- Make them truly extreme (uncomfortable is good)
- The value is in the discussion, not the provotype itself
- Look for the element of truth in each extreme

**Analogous Inspiration:**
- Go far from your domain (not competitor analysis)
- Abstract the problem first, then find analogies
- Extract the principle, don't copy the solution
